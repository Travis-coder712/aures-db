# CIS Tender 9 — Competitive Field Analysis

> ### Executive summary
>
> **Tender**: 5 GW NEM renewable generation, bids close 20 July 2026, results Nov 2026. **NSW EXCLUDED** (hit its CIS cap after T7). WA excluded (non-NEM). Effective states: **QLD, VIC, SA, TAS**. VIC ≥1.6 GW minimum + 470 MW cap on VIC solar-only; TAS ≥300 MW minimum; ~3.1 GW contestable pool open to ALL eligible states above their minimums. First Nations 500 MW set-aside. Target COD <end-2030.
>
> **Central story — the deliverability paradox**: T9 explicitly tightens MC2 (deliverability + timeline) and MC3 (org track record). Only **2 non-battery CIS generation projects had reached construction** by March 2026 (of ~69 awards) — Palmer Wind (Tilt SA, FID 9 Jan 2026 with 15-yr AGL PPA) was one. The T9 assessor is designing around a catastrophic conversion problem.
>
> **Prior CIS non-NSW winners** (35 projects, ~12 GW): all CISA-locked from rebidding the same project. Rebidding requires **formal written withdrawal notification to the Commonwealth** as an eligibility precondition (T9 Q&A Item 8, July 2026) — with AEMO track-record discretion applied as an additional scoring penalty even for compliant withdrawals, and Significant Event obligations under the Proforma CISA. Procedurally heavy. **Kentbruck Wind (VIC, 600 MW HMC/Illuma)** is the strongest voluntary-withdraw candidate given 15+ months pre-FID, but even for Kentbruck the machinery is a significant deterrent.
>
> **Top T9 picks under three lenses**:
> - **Lens 1 — Prior-CIS rebid candidates**: Kentbruck > Bell Bay > Goyder North. All conditional on DCCEEW permitting withdraw-and-resubmit. Likely contributes ~0 to actual T9 outcomes.
> - **Lens 2 — Gentailer + path-to-market**: **AGL Barn Hill Wind + BESS (SA, 300 MW wind + 270 MW BESS, AGL-owned since 2009, EPBC-approved, co-located)** — the standout T9 gentailer pitch. Plus Nonowie (Tilt, SA proven-delivery proxy), Iberdrola Three Rivers Solar (QLD), Guildford (Ark TAS captive), Alinta Yumali (SA long-shot on COD).
> - **Lens 3 — Earliest COD / MC2 credibility**: Fraser Coast Hybrid (QLD, 2027-09), Northern Midlands Solar (TAS, 2027-10), Solar River SB (SA, 2027-11), Colosseum Solar+BESS (QLD, 2028-01), Mount Hopeful Wind (QLD, 2028-06). QLD-dominated, mid-scale (250-400 MW).
>
> **Combined view — predicted T9 shape**:
> - **~2-3 GW** to mid-scale QLD Tier-1 (Lens 3 dominant pool — Callide, Mount Hopeful, Bulli Creek, Colosseum, Herries Range, Nyaninyuk, Tarong West)
> - **~1 GW** to proven-delivery proponents (Nonowie/Tilt, RWE fresh, possibly Edify/Windlab fresh)
> - **~500 MW** First Nations set-aside (watch Ark Energy/Korea Zinc)
> - **~1.5-2 GW** VIC state minimum (Meering West OR Warracknabeal + Nyaninyuk/Tall Tree)
> - **~300-500 MW** TAS state minimum (Guildford/Ark most likely)
> - **~0** rebids from stalled prior CIS awards (DCCEEW likely preserves scheme integrity)
> - **1 confirmed + 1-2 possible** gentailer wins (AGL Barn Hill is the anchor)
>
> **Structural finding**: The three lenses are largely orthogonal — no project appears on all three. T9 will look like T7 in shape (many mid-scale wins across many pure-play developers) with a modest deliverability premium favouring proven-track proponents. The tightened MC2/MC3 changes WHY specific projects win, not fundamentally WHO wins.
>
> **Standout T9 pitch to watch**: AGL/Barn Hill Wind + BESS. Cleanest single-owner, integrated, EPBC-approved wind+BESS in the field — the archetype T9's structure was designed to reward.

## 1. The T9 opportunity — corrected state math

CIS Tender 9 (federal, DCCEEW-mandated, run by AusEnergy Services Ltd) opened 25 May 2026 and closes for bids 20 July 2026, with results expected November 2026. Headline structure:

- **Volume**: 5 GW target renewable generation across the NEM
- **Region**: NSW **EXCLUDED** (NSW hit its CIS allocation cap after T7); WA excluded (non-NEM). Effective eligible states: **QLD, VIC, SA, TAS**
- **State minimums (NOT caps)**:
  - VIC **≥1.6 GW guaranteed minimum**
  - TAS **≥300 MW guaranteed minimum**
- **VIC solar-only cap**: 470 MW MAXIMUM on VIC solar-only projects (at Victoria's request — a demand-side constraint to protect Victorian solar developers from being fully crowded by cheap QLD solar)
- **Contestable pool**: ~3.1 GW open to QLD, SA, AND to VIC/TAS projects above their state minimums. So a VIC or TAS project that clears its state minimum still competes for additional wins from the contestable pool.
- **First Nations set-aside**: 500 MW for projects with ≥5% First Nations equity or revenue sharing
- **Target COD**: before end-2030
- **Contract**: CISA "cap & collar" — Annual Floor + Annual Ceiling. If NOR<Floor, Government pays min(90%×(Floor−NOR), Cap); if NOR>Ceiling, Operator repays min(50%×(NOR−Ceiling), Cap); between, zero settlement. Up to 15-yr Support Period. Floor 90% / Ceiling 50% sharing percentages are FIXED.

### State pool math (post-correction)

| Pool | Size | Contested by |
|---|---|---|
| VIC state minimum | ≥1.6 GW | VIC projects only (solar-only capped at 470 MW inside VIC) |
| TAS state minimum | ≥300 MW | TAS projects only |
| Contestable pool | ~3.1 GW | QLD + SA + VIC-above-min + TAS-above-min |

VIC and TAS wind projects effectively bid into TWO pools simultaneously — the state minimum, plus the contestable pool if they finish outside their state's guaranteed slice.

## 2. The deliverability paradox — central frame

The T9 changes materially tighten the deliverability scoring criteria. Read together, Clayton Utz (29 Aug 2025), Hamilton Locke (20 Aug 2025), and Modo Energy (27 May 2026) describe the same pattern:

- **MC2 (Project deliverability and timeline)** now dominates scoring, with a credible end-2030 COD as a hard merit lever
- **CISA departures** are now "take it or leave it" — material changes to the standard CISA contract are unlikely to be accepted. Non-standard bespoke contracts are excluded, not scored down.
- **Post-bid delivery-date changes** require Government notification
- **6-8 week bid window** (compressed from 9 months two-stage process to ~6 months single-stage) pressures marginal candidates and rewards developers with fresh T7/T8 process muscle memory

The consequence — and the paradox — is this:

- **Prior-round LOSERS** carry two conflicting signals into T9:
  - On paper they look mature (planning approvals advanced, connection progress, EPBC status) → strong on MC2 (<2030 COD credibility)
  - But they've been publicly shopped and haven't converted → weak on MC3 (organisational deliverability track record)
- **Prior-round WINNERS-BUT-STALLED** are CISA-locked on the same project (cannot rebid) — but their proponent's OTHER T9 bids will carry the stall as MC3 baggage
- **Fresh pipeline that hasn't been publicly shopped** carries no prior-round baggage, but must independently demonstrate deliverability capacity
- **Repeat winners with a delivery track record** (RWE post-MacIntyre, Tilt post-Palmer FID, Windlab in QLD) are structurally best-placed — proven MC3 credibility + fresh pipeline

### Sidebar — the "Zero for 15" delivery problem

RE / wind-watch reported 30 March 2026 that of the ~69 CIS projects awarded across all rounds to that date, **only 2 non-battery power generation projects had reached construction**. Palmer Wind (Tilt, SA, 274 MW T1) reached FID 9 January 2026 with a 15-year AGL PPA, becoming the first non-battery CIS generator to reach construction.

The Clean Energy Regulator has been publicly critical of the T1 conversion rate — most non-NSW T1 winners (Kentbruck, Hopeland, West Mokoan, Barwon) were still pre-FID at the 15-month mark. The T9 assessor is sitting on top of a scheme with a catastrophic conversion problem; the deliverability tightening in T9 is a direct response.

Source: RE 30 Mar 2026 "Zero for 15: Labor's flagship renewable scheme hits a standstill"; CER quarterly CIS reporting.

## 7. Three-lens synthesis — the most likely T9 winners

Three analytical lenses that stress-test the field. Each produces a different picking list; the intersection is instructive.

### Lens 1 — "Prior CIS wind winners that haven't progressed, most likely to rebid T9"

**Framing**: A CIS award triggers CISA negotiation. If the project can't reach the required commitments (planning, connection, financing) within the scheme's execution window, the CISA lapses and the project effectively frees up. Bowen has stated winners cannot ALTER CISA terms — but nothing prevents a lapsed-CISA project from rebidding a NEW tender under new terms. Projects that won a prior CIS but are visibly stalled at pre-FID are the natural candidates.

> **Note on eligibility** (hardened per CIS T9 Q&A Item 8, July 2026): Prior CIS winners are excluded from rebidding T9 on the same project unless the proponent has (a) formally notified the Commonwealth **in writing** of withdrawal from the prior CISA, **or** (b) received written confirmation from the Commonwealth that negotiations have ceased. Bowen's 24 June 2026 "welcome to withdraw and bid again" framing turned out to require substantially more machinery than the language implied. Three sharp implications:
>
> - **Formal written notification is a prerequisite for re-entry** — being in commercial discussions, publicly signalling intent, or simply not progressing does NOT satisfy the eligibility test. A proponent that read the LTESA R8 Q&A (permissive) and began preparing an LTESA bid without sending a formal written withdrawal is now reading T9 Q&A Item 8 with alarm.
> - **AEMO track-record discretion** — the Recommended Bids assessment may consider "track record of not executing binding commitments" — an additional scoring penalty even for formally compliant withdrawals.
> - **Withdrawal may constitute a Significant Event under the Proforma CISA** — triggering formal notification + remediation plan + prevention strategy obligations. Independent legal advice is recommended for any proponent contemplating withdrawal. The procedural weight makes withdrawal-and-resubmit a genuinely public event, not a discreet strategic pivot.
>
> Net: the withdraw-and-resubmit pathway is theoretically open but procedurally heavy. Kentbruck (HMC/Illuma) is still the strongest candidate given its 15+ month pre-FID drag, but even for Kentbruck the machinery is a significant deterrent. Expect Lens 1 to contribute ~0 to actual T9 wins. See companion note `cis-rebid-restrictions-hardening` (2026-07-07) for the full T9 Q&A Item 8 documentation.

## 8. What you may not have yet considered

1. **The deliverability paradox becomes concrete in the T1 conversion rate.** "Zero for 15" is not just a headline — CER quarterly reporting shows the pattern. T9's MC2/MC3 tightening is a direct response, which materially advantages developers with delivery muscle (Tilt post-Palmer, RWE post-MacIntyre) and disadvantages developers with prior public wins that stalled (HMC/Kentbruck, AGL/Hexham).

2. **AURES CIS_PROJECTS array is NSW-only** (9 entries — all NSW SW REZ / CWO REZ / New England). The frontend UI materially under-represents the actual T7 outcome (19 winners total). Extending this array with the 35 non-NSW winners we've now catalogued (Table A) is a natural bundled follow-up to this note.

3. **The 470 MW VIC solar cap is a demand-side constraint set by Victoria to protect its own solar developers**. It's not a soft ranking factor — VIC solar-only >470 MW is structurally shut out of the VIC allocation regardless of merit. It does NOT bind large VIC solar from bidding into the contestable pool from a QLD/SA project, but does bind VIC-based projects.

4. **First Nations 500 MW set-aside logistics**: FN partnerships are structurally harder to land for wind than solar (larger equity ask). Ark Energy (Korea Zinc) has previously shown strong FN engagement; Windlab and Neoen have partnerships on named projects. Any T9 bid targeting the FN set-aside needs a locked ≥5% equity/revenue partner BEFORE bid submission — this cannot be structured post-hoc.

5. **6-8 week bid window compression** favours developers with fresh T7/T8 process muscle memory. The compressed window essentially disqualifies developers who need to spin up bid teams from cold — advantage to Squadron, Ark, RWE, Acciona, Neoen, TagEnergy, Edify who have been in continuous CIS process for 12+ months.

6. **CISA reform (from T5 onward)** — commercial departures are no longer scored; unnecessary departures are an EXCLUSION risk (Clayton Utz 29 Aug 2025 — "take it or leave it"). Financier-heavy bidders that historically negotiated bespoke terms (infrastructure funds, some sovereign wealth) need to bid within the standard envelope or risk exclusion.

7. **VIC hybrid supply-side is heavily consumed by prior CIS wins**: 8 VIC hybrid projects hold T1/T4/T7 CISAs totalling ~2,238 MW. VIC T9 fresh hybrid capacity is essentially all-new proposals — the developed VIC hybrid pipeline has largely been sold to prior rounds.

8. **Barn Hill Wind + Barn Hill BESS co-location** — AGL holds the Barn Hill BESS (270 MW, planning submitted); Barn Hill Wind (300 MW, EPBC approved) is separately owned. A potential JV bid (BESS + wind on same site) would materially strengthen the T9 pitch on system-benefit scoring — but requires coordinated proponent agreement.

9. **Whyte Yarcowie 574 MW envelope vs 289 MW T7 award scope**: Wind Prospect's overall project envelope is 500-600 MW wind + BESS; T7 underwrote the 289 MW wind subset only. The remaining ~285 MW + BESS scope may be T9-eligible IF bid as a distinct project with its own connection point / SPV / commercial economics (see the NSW T8 EC10 "staged Project" precedent — analogous framework). Legal / connection question; not automatic.

10. **The "zero-for-15" delivery problem may pressure DCCEEW to allow rebids of stalled CISAs**. Bowen has publicly stated winners cannot alter CISA terms, but if T9 comes in materially under-subscribed on deliverable candidates, DCCEEW could open a specific rebid track. Watch for policy announcements between now and Nov 2026 results.

11. **Wind+BESS hybrids are NOT covered in this note per your scoping call** — a follow-on cut would surface projects like Cattle Creek Wind KCI (1,150 MW + 1,200 MWh, RWE, COD 2030-12), Mt Challenger Wind (216 MW + 720 MWh, Alinta) and the Barn Hill wind+BESS pairing option.
